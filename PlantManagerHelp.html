Getting Started
⚡

First Steps

Plant Manager is an offline-first, single-page app that stores all data in your browser's localStorage. No account is required to get started. Simply open tracker.tropicalrootsmaui.com and begin adding plants.

The recommended first steps are:

  • Add an Environment — create your grow tent, room, or outdoor space on the Environs tab.
  • Add a Mother Plant — go to the Plants tab and tap + Add Mother. Pick a strain from the catalog or type one in.
  • Set up Tasks — schedule watering, feeding, or pruning reminders so nothing slips through the cracks.
  • Export your data — once you have entries, use the gear menu to export a JSON backup file.
Tip All data lives in your browser. Clearing browser data or switching browsers will remove it unless you export a backup or enable Cloud Sync.

Plants
🌿

Mother Plants

The Plants tab is where you manage your mother plants. Each plant has a card showing its photo, health status badge, strain name, type, breeder, age, environment, growth stage, flowering dates, and clone success rate.

Adding a Plant

Tap + Add Mother and fill in the form. You can pick a strain from your Strain Library (which auto-fills strain, type, and breeder) or enter details manually. Fields include: strain name, strain type (indica/sativa/hybrid), breeder, genetics, source (seed/clone/tissue culture), date acquired, environment, growth stage, health status, photo, and notes.

Health Status

Each plant has a health status: Thriving, Healthy, Stressed, Sick, or Quarantine. This status appears as a colored badge on the card and is used for filtering.

Stage-Themed Card Stripes

Each plant card has a colored accent stripe across the top, color-coded by growth stage: lime/green for seedling and vegetative, gold/orange for flowering, violet for mature, muted gray for declining. Cards lift 3px and gain a subtle cyan glow on hover; the stripe brightens to full opacity. Lets you scan a grid of plants and see the lifecycle distribution at a glance.

Plant Card Actions

Each card has action buttons along the bottom:

  • Edit — modify any field. If you change the health status, a health check is auto-logged.
  • + Clone — log a clone batch from this mother.
  • + Task — create a task linked to this plant.
  • + Feed — log a feeding entry.
  • + Photo — add a journal photo.
  • + IPM — log a pest or disease issue.
  • 🔎 Identify — open the Pest Identification tool to snap a photo and identify pests or diseases.
  • + Harvest — record a harvest.
  • ❤ Health — log a 1–5 health score (see Health Check below).
  • 🏷 Tag — generate a printable QR code tag for this plant.
  • 📅 Timeline — view the full chronological event history combining feedings, photos, IPM, harvests, health checks, dry-backs, clones, and task completions.
  • ⚖ Dry-Back — log dry-back weight readings and view a line chart of weight over time.
  • 🌳 Lineage — view the full clone family tree visualization.
  • 📄 Report — generate a printable Plant Report with full grow history, stats, feedings, harvests, IPM log, and health checks.
  • 📈 Compare — select this plant for side-by-side comparison with 1–2 other plants.
  • 🔬 Trichome — log trichome readings (clear/cloudy/amber percentages) with harvest-window recommendations.
  • ✂ Training — log training events (topping, LST, SCROG, etc.) with technique badges and notes.
  • 🧬 Breed — log pollen collection, crosses, or seed harvests for this plant.
  • Archive — retire the plant from the active roster. You’re asked why (Harvested, Retired, Died, or Culled); Died and Culled feed the “money lost” figure in Strain Comparison. A toast lets you undo for a few seconds.

Environment Fields
  • Name — a descriptive name like "Veg Tent 4x4" or "Flower Room".

Clones

Clone Tracking

The Clones tab tracks clone batches taken from your mother plants. Each batch records how many cuttings were taken, how many rooted successfully, and how many failed.

Clone Batch Fields
  • Mother Plant — the source mother.
  • Date Taken — when cuttings were made.
  • Total Taken / Rooted / Failed — numeric counts.
  • Rooting Status — Rooting, Rooted, Transplanted, or Failed.
  • Medium — Rockwool, Rapid Rooter, Soil, Perlite, Aero, or Water.
  • Notes — free-text observations.
Stats & Progress

The top of the tab shows aggregate stats: total taken, total rooted, total failed, total batches, and overall success rate. Each batch card shows a color-coded progress bar (green = rooted, red = failed, gray = pending).

Clone success rates automatically appear on the corresponding mother plant cards on the Plants tab.

Clone QR Tags

Tap the 🏷 Tag button on any clone card to generate a printable QR label. Clone tags include the mother plant reference, strain with generation label, cutting date, rooted/failed/total counts, success rate, and rooting status. Print all clone tags at once from the gear menu via Clone Tags. The QR payload uses the TRM-C format and can be decoded with the QR Lookup tool.

Photo Journal
📸

Photo Journal

Build a visual timeline of each plant's growth. Upload photos with growth stage tags and notes. Photos are stored locally as compressed base64 images (auto-resized to 400px max dimension).

Adding a Photo

Tap + Add Photo, select a plant, upload an image (three ways — see below), choose the growth stage (Seedling, Vegetative, Flowering, Harvest, Curing), and add optional notes.

Three ways to attach an image
  • Click to pick — the original flow. Tap the preview tile to open the file picker.
  • Drag & drop — on desktop, drag any image file onto the preview tile. A cyan dashed outline confirms the drop target while you drag.
  • Paste from clipboard — press Ctrl+V (or Cmd+V) anywhere in the modal to paste a screenshot or copied image. Especially handy for screenshots taken with Win+Shift+S or Cmd+Shift+4.

You can also send a photo into the Journal from outside the app on Android: see Receive shared photos in the Install section.

EXIF Date Detection

When you upload a photo, Plant Manager reads the photo's EXIF DateTimeOriginal from the original file before resizing. If a capture date is found, the journal entry's timestamp uses that date instead of the upload date — so a photo you took last Tuesday lands on Tuesday in the timeline, not today. A small ✓ Using photo capture date: ... chip confirms detection during upload. The resized version stored in the app contains no EXIF data (canvas resize strips metadata).

EXIF Orientation Auto-Correction

Many phone cameras (especially Android) save portrait photos with landscape pixel data and an EXIF Orientation tag instructing viewers to rotate. Plain canvas.drawImage() ignores that tag, which used to leave portrait shots showing up sideways in the journal. Plant Manager now reads the Orientation tag (values 1–8) and applies the appropriate rotation, flip, or transpose to the canvas before re-encoding the JPEG — so portrait photos always land right-side up regardless of how the camera stored them.

Photo Lightbox

Tap any photo in the grid to open a full-screen lightbox viewer. Features:

  • Prev/next arrows on either side cycle through photos in the current filtered view
  • Click the image to toggle 2× zoom (cursor changes from zoom-in to zoom-out)
  • Swipe left/right on touch devices to navigate
  • Arrow keys (←/→) on desktop
  • Caption below the image shows plant name, capture date, growth stage, and notes
  • Counter at the bottom shows position (e.g. “3 / 8”)
  • Delete shortcut in the top-left removes the current photo (with toast-undo)
  • Esc, click on backdrop, or tap the × button to close
Filter

Use the plant dropdown to filter photos to a single plant. The lightbox respects the current filter, so prev/next walks through whichever subset you’re viewing.

IPM — Pest & Disease
🛡

IPM Log

The IPM (Integrated Pest Management) tab tracks pest sightings, disease outbreaks, and treatment applications.

Issue Fields
  • Pest/Disease — choose from 11 common types: aphids, spider mites, fungus gnats, whiteflies, thrips, powdery mildew, bud rot, root rot, nutrient burn, nutrient deficiency, or other.
  • Severity — Low, Medium, High, or Critical.
  • Plant & Environment — link to the affected plant and/or environment.
  • Treatment Applied — pick from a catalog of 60+ IPM products organized by category: insecticides, miticides, fungicides, biologicals, organic remedies, KNF treatments, and environmental controls.
  • Photo & Notes — document what you observe.
Inline Pest Identification

The IPM form includes a built-in Pest Identification section with 4 buttons: Snap Photo (camera/upload), Auto-ID (Google Cloud Vision AI — requires API key), Lens (opens Google Lens for manual lookup), and Guide (opens the Visual Pest Guide). When Auto-ID identifies a pest, tap Apply to auto-fill the pest dropdown, issue type, and severity without leaving the form.

Active vs Resolved

Issues start as Active and can be marked Resolved when the problem is handled. Use the status filter to view only active or resolved issues.

Dashboard Integration

Active IPM issues show as a count on the Dashboard stat cards. The number helps you track unresolved pest pressure at a glance.

Strains & Catalog
🧬

Strain Library

The Strains tab is your personal strain library plus a built-in catalog of 360+ strains from Maui-based breeders.

Your Strains

Manually add strains or import them from the catalog. Each strain card shows name, type (indica/sativa/hybrid), breeder, genetics, flowering time, and average review rating if you have reviews.

Browse Catalog

The built-in catalog includes strains from 7 breeders: Sticky Finger Seeds, Hawaiian Budline, Ziplock Seeds, Makena Genetics, Deep Green Genetics, Aloha Island Genetics, and CloneFreak (clones only). Filter by breeder, search by name, and quick-add strains to your library.

Seed Bank

Access the Seed Bank from the Strains toolbar or the Dashboard. Track seed packets with strain name, breeder, count, type (feminized/regular/autoflower), source, purchase date, germination rate, and notes. The Browse Catalog button inside the Seed Bank lets you quick-add seed entries from the strain catalog (excludes clone-only providers). Features include: low stock warnings (⚠), seed age tracking with viability alerts, germination stats summary, “Plant Next” suggestion scoring, sort controls, and a Print Labels button to generate QR seed packet labels.

Strain Reviews

Tap the ⭐ Reviews button on any strain card to write a personal grow review. Reviews capture:

  • Overall rating (1–5 stars)
  • Grow difficulty, yield rating, and actual flowering time
  • Grow method, effects, terpene/flavor notes
  • Grow tips and general notes
  • Optional photo

Average ratings display directly on strain cards so you can compare strains at a glance.

Tip When adding a plant on the Plants tab, the strain picker auto-fills type and breeder from your library. Build your strain library first for faster plant entry.

After loading demo data

Every record created by the demo fixture is tagged internally with a _demo:true flag at build time. This lets the app distinguish demo records from real ones, so when you’re ready to start tracking your own grow, you can remove the demo cleanly without touching anything you’ve added yourself.

When demo data is detected, a banner appears at the top of the dashboard (between the hero and stats grid) with three actions:

  • Clear demo data — opens a confirmation modal listing the per-category record counts that will be removed (e.g. plants −6, harvests −5, feedings −41, ...). On confirm, only _demo:true records are removed — your own data is preserved.
  • + Add my plant — opens the Add Mother Plant form. The demo records stay in place alongside your new plant. Useful if you want to keep the demo around as a reference while you build your own grow.
  • Keep mixed — hides the banner permanently for this session (saved in localStorage as demoBannerDismissed). The demo records stay loaded but the prompt stops nagging. You can restore the banner anytime via Command Palette → Show Demo Banner.

The Clear Demo Data action is also reachable independently of the banner via:

  • The gear menu → 🧹 Clear Demo Data
  • The Command Palette: Ctrl+K → "clear demo"

All three paths invoke the same confirmation flow. Users who loaded demo data before v2.22.0 (when the _demo flag didn’t exist yet) get auto-tagged on first launch under the new version by a legacy-detection backfill that recognizes records with notes:'(demo)' — so the Clear command works retroactively without manual cleanup.

Data & Backup
💾

Data Management

Local Storage

All data is stored in your browser's localStorage under the key _trm_pm. This means data persists between sessions but is tied to the specific browser and device you use.

Export

Go to gear menu → Export Data to download a complete JSON backup. The file includes a checksum for integrity verification. Export regularly — it only takes a second and protects all your grow records.

Backup Nudge

A passive 💾 indicator appears in the header when your last export is getting stale: amber at 14+ days (or never exported), red at 30+ days. Click it to jump straight to Export Data. Hides itself when backups are fresh or when you have no data yet to back up. The thresholds are configurable in source if you want them sooner or later.

Import (with diff preview)

Go to gear menu → Import Data to restore from a JSON backup. Before anything is applied, a diff preview modal shows a per-category record count comparison (current vs backup, with Δ) across mothers, environments, clones, tasks, feedings, journal photos, IPM logs, harvests, strains, env readings, seeds, and health checks. Backup metadata (export date, app version) is shown at the top with a version-mismatch warning when applicable, and a red “heads up” appears if the backup contains fewer total records than your current data — protects against accidentally clobbering data with an older backup. Confirm to apply, Cancel to abort.

Undo via Toast

Destructive actions (archive plant, archive environment, delete task, delete photo) skip the “Are you sure?” modal and run immediately. A 6-second toast appears in the bottom-right with an Undo button. Click Undo to restore the prior state; ignore it and the action persists. Faster, reversible, and respects your flow. The undo restores the full record snapshot including any associated activity-log entry.

Cloud Sync (Firebase)

Tap the ☁ Cloud Sync button in the header to connect with Firebase. Cloud sync provides automatic backup and cross-device synchronization. Your data is encrypted and stored securely. Log in with your email to enable sync.

Restore from cloud uses the same diff preview modal as file imports: before any destructive overwrite, you see a per-category record-count comparison (current vs cloud backup, with Δ), the backup’s export date, a version-mismatch warning when applicable, and a red “heads up” if the cloud copy has fewer records than your current local data. Confirm with Restore from cloud to apply, Cancel to abort — you’ll never accidentally clobber your local data with an older cloud snapshot without seeing what’s about to happen.

Encrypted Exports

Go to gear menu → 🔒 Export Encryption to toggle encrypted exports on or off. When enabled, Export Data will prompt for a password and encrypt your backup using AES-256-GCM with PBKDF2 key derivation (SHA-256, 600,000 iterations). The export is saved as a .encrypted.json file. On import, encrypted files are auto-detected by their header and a password prompt appears. Unencrypted backups are always accepted regardless of this setting. A password strength indicator helps you choose a strong password. There is no password recovery — if you forget the password, the backup cannot be restored.

Manual install (fallback)

  • Desktop Chrome/Edge — click the install icon in the address bar, or three-dot menu → Install Tropical Roots Maui - Plant Manager
  • iOS Safari — Share button → Add to Home Screen (same as the walkthrough above)

Accessibility
♿

Accessibility Features

Plant Manager is built to work for people who navigate by keyboard, screen reader, reduced-motion preference, or high-contrast vision. Specifics:

Keyboard Navigation

Every interactive element is reachable via Tab. Focus is indicated by a 2px cyan outline that matches the brand (visible only via :focus-visible, so it doesn’t appear on mouse clicks). Tab dropdowns and the command palette can be operated entirely from the keyboard — see the Keyboard Shortcuts section for the full layer.

Skip-to-Content Link

The first focusable element on every page is a hidden Skip to main content link. Press Tab on first load to reveal it; Enter jumps focus past the header and tab nav directly to the dashboard content. Useful for keyboard and screen-reader users who don’t want to walk through navigation links every page.

Screen Reader Support

Icon-only buttons (gear, weather, moon, cloud-sync, theme-toggle, modal-close, backup-nudge) all carry aria-label attributes describing their purpose. The toast container has role="status" with aria-live="polite" so success and error messages get announced. The settings menu has aria-haspopup and aria-expanded that update as it opens and closes. Top-level landmarks use semantic <header role="banner">, <nav role="navigation">, and <main role="main">.

Modals & Dialogs

All modal dialogs in the app (forms, confirms, calculators, the photo lightbox, command palette, About, Changelog viewer) carry role="dialog", aria-modal="true", and aria-labelledby pointing at the modal title so screen readers announce them correctly. A focus trap keeps Tab and Shift+Tab cycling inside the open modal so keyboard users can’t accidentally tab out into background content. On open, focus moves to the first form field (or the first action button if there are no fields); on close, focus is restored to whatever element opened the modal, so keyboard users continue right where they left off instead of being dumped at the top of the page.

Reduced Motion

If your operating system sets prefers-reduced-motion: reduce, Plant Manager honors it. All transitions are reduced to near-instant, hover-lift effects on cards are disabled, the stat-card icon scale-rotate animation is disabled, the fadeUp panel-transition is disabled, and the pulse animation is disabled. The cyan focus ring still works.

Haptic Feedback (Mobile)

On devices that support navigator.vibrate, Plant Manager fires a brief tactile pulse on a couple of key actions so the interaction feels physical: a 10ms tick when you press Undo on a toast (so the undo lands before the toast fades), and a 15ms tick when you mark a task as complete. No-op on desktop and on devices where the OS blocks vibration; the parent action runs identically either way.

Contrast (WCAG AA)

Body text and secondary text both pass WCAG AA contrast ratios on small body copy in both dark and light themes. Tertiary/dim text (used for captions, timestamps, metadata) sits at the AA threshold and is bumped from earlier versions to ensure readability.

Reporting Issues

If you hit a screen reader, keyboard, or contrast issue that this section doesn’t cover, the GitHub repo accepts accessibility reports as issues: github.com/magikh0e/PlantManager/issues. Tag with the a11y label.

Frequently Asked Questions
❓

Common Questions

The most-asked questions about Plant Manager — tap any question to expand the answer.

Where is my data stored, and is it private?
All Plant Manager data lives in your browser's localStorage on your own device. Nothing is uploaded, tracked, or shared with any server — there is no analytics, no telemetry, no account creation. Cloud Sync is entirely optional and uses your own Firebase configuration when enabled, meaning the data still belongs to you and lives in infrastructure you control.
Will I lose my data if I clear my browser cache?
Yes. Clearing site data, switching browsers, switching devices, or uninstalling the PWA without backing up first will erase your plants, journals, feeding logs, and harvests. Always export a JSON backup regularly via the gear menu → Export Data, or enable Cloud Sync for automatic protection. For sensitive data, enable Export Encryption to password-protect your backups.
Is Plant Manager free to use?
Yes. Plant Manager is fully free and open-source under the GNU GPL v3 license. There are no accounts, no advertisements, no paid tiers, no premium features behind a paywall, and no usage telemetry. The full source is in a single HTML file you can read, modify, and self-host.
I loaded demo data. How do I clear it out now that I'm tracking real plants?
Every record the demo fixture creates is tagged internally with _demo:true. To remove all demo records without touching your own data, you have three options: (1) click Clear demo data in the banner that appears at the top of the dashboard when demo records are detected, (2) open the gear menu and click 🧹 Clear Demo Data, or (3) hit Ctrl+K and search for "clear demo." All three paths show the same confirmation modal listing the per-category record counts that will be removed before deleting anything. Records you've added yourself are preserved.
I dismissed the demo banner with "Keep mixed." How do I get it back?
Open the Command Palette (Ctrl+K or /) and search for "Show Demo Banner". That clears the demoBannerDismissed flag and the banner reappears on the dashboard next time you visit. Alternatively, just clear demo data directly from the gear menu — the banner is no longer relevant once demo records are gone.
How does Plant Manager compare to other grow tracker apps?
Most other cultivation trackers (e.g. Grow with Jane) are freemium with subscription tiers, ads in the free tier, and cloud-locked data on the vendor's servers. Plant Manager is the opposite: free forever, no ads, no subscription, and your data stays on your device unless you explicitly opt into your own Firebase sync. It's also open-source under GPL-3.0, which means you can read, fork, or self-host the entire app. For a detailed feature-by-feature comparison, see Plant Manager vs Grow with Jane.
How are the plant costs in Strain Comparison calculated?
Every expense you log is one of two kinds: a direct cost (tagged to a specific plant when you added it) or shared overhead (untagged — tents, meters, electricity, a bulk soil order, and so on). Shared overhead is split equally across every plant you've grown, so each plant carries its own direct expenses plus one equal slice of overhead.

For each strain those per-plant costs are then grouped by what happened to the plant:
  • Cost (harvested) — total cost of the plants that actually produced a harvest.
  • Lost (dead plants) — money spent on plants you archived as Died or Culled that never yielded — your sunk cost.
  • Cost/Plant — the strain's total cost divided by every plant of that strain (harvested, dead, retired, or still growing).
  • Cost/Gram — the cost of the harvested plants divided by their total dry weight. Dead-plant losses are shown separately, not baked in.
The Died / Culled distinction comes from the reason you choose when you archive a plant (Harvested / Retired / Died / Culled). Plants archived before this feature existed, or without a reason, still count toward totals but are not treated as losses.

Note: the Cost per Gram by Strain chart on the Analytics tab uses a simpler method — it splits shared overhead by each strain's share of total yield rather than per plant — so its $/g figure can differ slightly from the one in Strain Comparison.
Does Plant Manager work offline?
Yes. After the page loads once, the service worker caches the entire app plus all assets so you can open and use it without an internet connection. This is especially handy in grow tents or rooms with weak Wi-Fi. When your device loses connectivity, a small amber Offline pill appears in the header so you always know — your edits keep saving to local storage and will sync when you reconnect (if Cloud Sync is enabled). Help, comparison, privacy, and changelog pages are all precached too. Install it as a Progressive Web App (see the Install as App section above) for the best offline experience.
How do I install Plant Manager on my phone?
The app captures the install moment automatically — on Android (Chrome/Edge) you’ll see a one-time toast at the bottom of the screen offering Install, and tapping it fires the native install dialog. On iPhone/iPad Safari, where browsers don’t expose an install event, the app shows a one-time walkthrough modal: tap the Share button at the bottom of Safari → scroll down to Add to Home Screen → tap Add. You can re-trigger either prompt at any time from the gear menu → 📲 Install App or Command Palette → “Install App.” Once installed, the app launches in its own window without browser chrome and works fully offline. See the Install as App section above for the manual-install fallback paths.
Can I sync data between my phone and computer?
Yes, two ways:
  • Manual: Export a JSON backup from one device, import it on the other via the gear menu.
  • Automatic: Enable Cloud Sync with your own Firebase project to keep multiple devices in sync. Encryption is optional on both manual exports and cloud backups.
How do I differentiate multiple plants of the same strain?
When you add a second plant with the same strain name, both are auto-numbered — you'll see "Blue Dream #1" and "Blue Dream #2" across all dropdowns, charts, and logs. You can also set a custom nickname per plant (e.g. "Pele" or "North Tent") which overrides the number; the display becomes "Blue Dream — Pele". Need a name idea? Click the 🎲 dice button next to the nickname field for a random Polynesian/Hawaiian name suggestion (66 available from mythology, aliʻi lineage, and nature; won't repeat names already in use).
Can I encrypt my data exports?
Yes. Open the gear menu and select Export Encryption to enable password-based encryption. When enabled, exports prompt you for a password (with strength indicator) and write a .encrypted.json file. Encryption uses AES-256-GCM with PBKDF2 key derivation (600,000 SHA-256 iterations) for brute-force resistance. On import, encrypted files are auto-detected and prompt for the decryption password. Password recovery is not possible — store your password somewhere safe.
Which devices and browsers are supported?
Plant Manager works on any modern browser:
  • Mobile: iOS Safari 14+, Android Chrome, Brave, Firefox
  • Desktop: Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari, Brave
  • Installable as a PWA on all of the above
Internet Explorer is not supported. Mobile is the recommended interface for grow-room logging.
How accurate is the harvest yield estimate?
Harvest estimates are derived from your logged plant height, age, growth stage, environment readings (VPD, light hours, CO₂), and historical yields per strain. Accuracy improves over time as you log more harvests — the system learns your specific genetics and growing conditions. Treat early estimates as rough guidance, not guarantees. Final yields depend on many factors beyond the model: pheno selection, dry-back schedule, training, IPM events, and post-harvest curing.
Can I print plant tags for the grow room?
Yes. Three options:
  • Single tag: Open any plant card → "More" dropdown → QR Tag
  • Batch: Gear menu → Tags & Labels → Print Plant Tags / Clone Tags / Breeding Cross Tags
  • Reports: Plant Report PDF includes a tag for hand-off or archiving
Each tag includes a scannable QR code that opens the plant directly in the app.
